# Mathematician-Style Solution Guideline

Write solutions as short proofs, not as fragments of intuition.

## Core Standard

1. State the claim precisely before arguing for it.
2. Introduce notation explicitly and use it consistently.
3. Begin from definitions, not from vague intuition.
4. Justify every nontrivial step by a definition, theorem, or counting principle.
5. Write in complete sentences; formulas should support the argument, not replace it.
6. Keep the proof linear: each sentence should advance the argument.
7. Distinguish clearly between what is fixed, what varies, and what is being counted or proved.
8. End with a clear concluding sentence that matches the original question.

## Preferred Structure

1. Restate the target.
2. Recall the relevant definition.
3. Apply the appropriate principle step by step.
4. Conclude in one sentence.

## Short Template

> We claim that ____.  
> By definition, ____.  
> For each fixed ____, there are exactly ____ choices for ____.  
> Therefore, by ____, the total number is ____.  
> Hence ____.

## Style Rules

- Prefer precise words such as “for each,” “there exists,” “exactly,” and “therefore.”
- Avoid conversational phrases such as “it is obvious” unless the statement is truly immediate.
- Avoid unexplained jumps.
- Do not leave the conclusion implicit.
- Keep the solution concise, but never at the expense of rigor.
